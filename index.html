About Quake Map

This application was designed and developed by Esri's Prototype Lab in Redlands, California. The application is built using the following technologies:

  • ArcGIS API for JavaScript (beta) by Esri
  • D3.js by Mike Bostock
  • Google Fonts, specifically Roboto and Berkshire Swash
  • jQuery by the jQuery Foundation Inc.
  • Sylvester by James Coglan

Earthquake data consists of 10,835 seimic events from 1900 to July 2015 with magnitudes of six or greater on the Richter scale. The data was downloaded from the USGS's ANSS Comprehensive Earthquake Catalog. Information on the largest and deadliest earthquakes was sourced from wikipedia.

Seimic events hightlighted red in the chart indicate those events within a one year period, or half a year either side of the red line.

Quake Map Tutorial (1 of 3)

The quake chart, the primary tool for navigating a century of seismic events in both time and space. Each dot represents a seismic event between 1900 and late July 2015, there are a total of 10,835 represented in this chart. The horizontal and vertical axises represents the date and magnitude of the seismic event.

The red graticle is used to select a subset of seismic events for display on the globe and information panel on the right hand side. The graticle can be moved by dragging it with the left mouse button pressed or by simply click any other event on the chart. The selection window is one year, or half a year either side of the red graticle. Moving your mouse over a selected event will highlight the corresponding entry on the globe and information panel.
